CONTEXT

A Client Project

In our UI/UX class, my team had the incredible opportunity to collaborate with a Google Ventures startup of our choice to address a client-provided problem. While the project ended up being highly collaborative at all stages, I played a key role in designing the lo-fi wireframes, high-fi mockups, and prototypes, and I took full responsibility for developing the style guide.

What is Zest?

Zest is a B2B corporate gifting integration used by brands to allow gift purchasers maximize product range, discounts, and customization options. In order for users to reach this site, the gift purchaser visits a brand‚Äôs website, finds the corporate gifting landing page usually embedded in the footer, and opens the corporate gift shop powered by Zest.

PROBLEM

Need for a New Shipping Method

Currently, Zest offers two shipping methods: shipping to recipients' addresses and sending e-gifts. However, users frequently needed the option to ship their entire order to a single address, which is not available, and led them to contact customer service to manually sort things out.

The two most common users in need of this new shipping method are:

Our task was to incorporate this feature seamlessly into the existing interface.

üîî The Big Question

How might we ensure that corporate and bulk gift customers know and understand the delivery options and can easily distinguish between the methods in the corporate gift shop to select the delivery that works best for them?

PROCESS

Sketches

We began by individually brainstorming different approaches to implementing bulk shipping.

After reviewing our sketches as a team, we identified several key patterns we wanted to incorporate into the designs:

  1. The bulk shipping option should naturally be placed in the Delivery Method section.
  2. Selecting bulk shipping should trigger a change in the interface.
  3. A sticky cart with a clear per-recipient total should be included for transparency

However, there were still questions around how users should select the bulk shipping option. Our ideas ranged from using a simple drop-down to differentiating between e-gift and physical shipping before narrowing down to bulk or individual shipments.

Lo-fi

When discussing where to place the bulk shipping option, we considered creating a third delivery method but felt this was an overly simplistic solution that didn't fit seamlessly into the existing interface. Instead, we chose to incorporate it under the 'ship to recipients‚Äô addresses' option to maintain consistency.

We updated the recipients‚Äô list to exclude multiple addresses, as this naturally followed from shipping the entire order to one location.

Additionally, I suggested adding a sticky shopping cart during our previous sketching session to address a potential pain point: users currently have to click 'Review and Checkout' to trigger a modal that displays their order total. By implementing a sticky cart, we would be able to reduce unnecessary back-and-forth navigation.

Client Feedback

After sharing our lo-fi wireframes with Zest‚Äôs founding designer, Christine, her feedback prompted us to reconsider our designs.

‚ùå Iteration 1

We initially put the bulk shipping option under the existing "ship to recipients' addresses" as a way of grouping both physical delivery methods. However, Christine's feedback informed us that the new bulk shipping method operates more differently than we thought, so grouping it with the existing physical delivery method would not make sense.

‚úÖ Iteration 2

As a result, we added a third delivery method in our hi-fi. Having a drop-down of a ship to multiple addresses vs. ship to one address not only required more clicks but could also lead to confusing behavior after the selection. Therefore, a third option provided clarity and reduced friction in the user experience.

Identity

SOLUTION

Final Designs

01 | Sticky Shopping Cart

Users can view their order summary and have an estimate of what their total is before moving onto the next step, Review and Checkout. This eliminates the need to go back and forth between pages to edit the gift order.

Furthermore, we enhanced user interaction by incorporating features such as the ability to remove items from the cart upon hover.

02 | Bulk Shipping

New delivery method that allows gift purchasers to ship multiple packages to one address, usually themselves.

LEARNINGS

Takeaways

  1. Expand your scope but maintain focus: While it's essential to be flexible and adapt the project scope as needed, keeping the project within defined parameters helps in maintaining focus and managing complexity. This balance was crucial in integrating the new bulk shipping feature effectively while respecting the existing user experience.
  2. Keep collaborators informed: Regularly update all team members and stakeholders to optimize time and effort. Keeping Christine, especially, and Jeremy informed throughout the process ensured that our designs were on the right track and allowed us to easily incorporate their feedback.
  3. Prioritize user testing: User feedback is incredibly valuable for validating and refining designs. Although our project timeline was limited, and we couldn‚Äôt conduct user testing, we recognized its importance through feedback from our client.
