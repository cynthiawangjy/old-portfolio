PROBLEM

An Outdated Website

Imagine wanting to try out a new bakery and visiting their website just to see this:

As a child, I frequently visited Edelweiss Bakery with my family for their pastries and signature fruit flan, which was a staple at our birthday celebrations. When I finally checked out their website not long ago, I immediately decided to embark on a redesign.

After performing a quick audit on the website, I identified several key issues I wanted to focus on:

PROCESS

Wireframing

I focused on redesigning the website's layout to follow more of a grid structure and enhancing the visual hierarchy for better readability. I prioritized key navigation items and moved the less critical ones to a hamburger menu to reduce clutter.

I also reduced the layout from three columns to two to improve readability and better highlight the images. The images themselves needed updating due to poor quality and lighting.

Balancing Feedback

While working on the hi-fi version of the website, a friend pointed out that my design felt more suited to a modern bakery rather than the family-owned, homely vibe of Edelweiss Bakery.

This raised a crucial question:

How can I maintain the essence of the original website?

After discussing this with another designer, I learned that preserving the old website's essence was challenging due to its numerous issues. To address both perspectives, I created a style guide that incorporated some existing elements from the old site, such as a script font and select colors, to better reflect the bakery's original character.

Identity

I developed a comprehensive style guide that retained elements of the old website‚Äôs warm color palette and typography. The new design utilizes only two fonts, with Kaushan Script used sparingly for the logo and hero text to reinforce Edelweiss's brand identity. Additionally, the design streamlined the user interface by reducing the number of button types from 7 to 2, enhancing both simplicity and consistency.

SOLUTION

Final Designs

The final design reflects a significant improvement in usability, aesthetics, and accessibility. Despite challenges in balancing the old website's essence with modern design elements, the redesigned interface successfully communicates the bakery's story and ethos to its audience.

I was able to bring the accessibility score from an 80 to a 97 by adding alternative text to all of my images and using proper HTML tags when coding.

LEARNINGS

Takeaways

  1. Balancing tradition with modernization: One of the biggest challenges was blending Edelweiss's traditional charm with a modern look. While the redesign is quite different from the original, I made sure to incorporate some of the original brand colors and typography to maintain that connection to their roots.
  2. Need for user testing: More user testing would have been beneficial to ensure that the redesign felt consistent with the bakery's original vibe rather than creating a sense of two separate bakeries.
  3. Great practice: This was a great opportunity to enhance both my coding and UI design skills. I had so much fun working on this redesign!
